Ekadashi

The day of Ekadashi holds great significance for Vaishnavites. In a lunar month, this day occurs twice: once during the dark fortnight and once during the light fortnight. It's advised to abstain from food and liquids for the full day on Ekadashi. Scriptures claim that, on Ekadasi, sin is found in cereal grains. Therefore, on this day, one should refrain from eating grains and lentils. On Ekadasi, Vaishnavites fast for the delight of Lord Krishna. This is the reason knowing about the Ekadashi calendar is crucial for you.

Things to do on Ekadashi Day:

  • Wake up early and take a shower. Stay calm and relax your mind and body.
  • Do not eat grains and lentils.
  • Give maximum time for spiritual activities like chanting the Holy Name, reading and hearing about the glories of The Lord.
  • If it is not possible to remain empty stomach,then one can have fruits or Ekadasi food prasad.
  • If have unintentionally missed Ekadashi, you can also fast on the next day of Ekadashi and discontinue fast on the third day. To avoid such mistakes, stay updated with Ekadashi calendar always.
  • Do not sleep in the daytime and also avoid shaving and oil massage this day.

  • Always make sure to break the fast during the Paran Time.
  • Also, avoid having heavy food initially as your stomach was empty during fasting and it might pain your stomach to eat heavy food all of sudden. Make sure to start a day with fresh fruit juices or light food.
  • Also, make sure to not overeat a day before fasting as it might become difficult for you to stay empty stomach on the day of fasting.

Why is Ekadashi Fasting good for you?

Good for digestion:

Fasting is beneficial for your health in addition to its spiritual benefits, which are undeniable on this auspicious day of Ekadashi. Your body and mind feel more relaxed than on other days since you don't feed your body hefty meals three times a day on the fasting day.

Connects your soul with the super soul:

People who fast on Ekadashi must spend the entire day remembering supreme power in order to atone for their sins and negative karma. Your fast may be ineffective if you don't think about God. Thus, if you fast with a pure heart, you can become one with the super soul.

Liberation from the life and death cycle:

Hindu sages hold that if you observe the Ekadashi fast without any sense of greed, it can help you break free from the vast cycles of life and death.

What is the purpose of celebrating Ekadashi?

Ekadashi is celebrated on the eleventh day of every lunar fortnight. The name comes from the Sanskrit words "eka," which means "one," and "Dasha," which means "ten." In the Hindu religion, this day holds great spiritual significance as it is considered a day for introspection, meditation, and spiritual communication with Lord Krishna, the holy Supreme Personality of Godhead.

Ekadashi is a time to take a vacation from everyday life to cleanse one's body, mind, and soul. By avoiding certain foods (grains, lentils, beans, etc.) and partaking in spiritual activities, devotees hope to increase their consciousness and become more intimate with the divine energies that govern the universe.

What not to do on Ekadashi?

When you begin this holy path of Ekadashi observance, keep in mind that there are some practices that are off limits on this day. During the Ekadashi fast, it is customary to refrain from consuming grains, beans, and specific vegetables. The core of Ekadashi is a deliberate, methodical dedication to self-purification.
