About Founder

A C BHAKTIVEDANTA SRILA PRABHUPADA

Shrila Prabhupada, born Abhay Charan De, was a highly revered spiritual leader and the founder-acharya of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON). Born in 1896 in Calcutta, India, he dedicated his life to spreading the teachings of Bhakti yoga and the practice of devotion to Lord Krishna. In 1965, at the age of 69, Shrila Prabhupada embarked on a historic journey to the United States with a mission to share the timeless wisdom of the Bhagavad Gita and revive the authentic teachings of the Vedic scriptures. Through his tireless efforts, he established over 100 temples worldwide, authored numerous books, including translations of sacred texts, and inspired a global movement of spiritual seekers. His profound impact on the lives of millions and his dedication to fostering a deep connection with the divine continue to be celebrated, making Shrila Prabhupada a revered figure in the realm of spiritual leadership.


HARE KRISHNA MOVEMENT

Srila Prabhupada battled alone for the first year after coming to New York City in September 1965 in order to develop his Krishna-conscious movement. He led a modest life, gave lectures whenever and whenever he could, and eventually some tiny interest in his teachings was generated. Despite continuing to operate out of a modest Lower East Side storefront in New York City, Srila Prabhupada still established a global spiritual community in July 1966. He called it the International Society for Krishna Consciousness or ISKCON for short and thus began the Hare Krishna movement in America.

Festivals of ISKCON

ISKCON (International Society for Krishna Consciousness) temples celebrate a variety of festivals throughout the year, each serving as a joyous occasion for devotees to come together, express devotion, and deepen their spiritual connection. Some of the major festivals observed in ISKCON temples include:

Harinam Sankirtan

Krishna and His name, both are similar. It is because Lord Krishna has put all his divine powers in His name. Chanting the holy name of Lord Krishna collectively means coming together and spreading the message of togetherness.

Ekadashi

The day of Ekadashi holds great significance for Vaishnavites. In a lunar month, this day occurs twice: once during the dark fortnight and once during the light fortnight. It's advised to abstain from food and liquids for the full day on Ekadashi.

Gopashtami

It is believed that on Gopastami, Lord Krishna became a skilled cowherd. Previously, He used to handle the calves only. In the month of Kartika, the 8th lunar day is considered as the Gopastami by the associate authorities.

Janmashtami

The celebrations at ISKCON revolve around the all-encompassing Lord Krishna, and Janmashtami is perhaps the biggest of them all. ISKCON Janmashtami celebration happens all across the world with great Devotion. Kindly Click on 'Continue Reading' to know more.

Radhashtami

It was on this day, almost 5,000 years ago, that Srimati Radharani made her beautiful debut on Earth. All of us are drawn to her because she is the holy feminine spirit of Lord Krishna, His loving potency. Srimati Radharani is the queen of all living things as well as the queen of Lord Krishna's heart.

Ram Navami

It is thought that the almighty Lord appears to drive out the devils whenever and wherever irreligion arises and attempts to reject the principles of our faith. This is the main concept of Ram Navami celebrations. The most well-known Krishna incarnation is Lord Ram.
